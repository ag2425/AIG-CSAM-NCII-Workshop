Community Building for Researchers on Generative AI-Facilitated Image-Based Abuse

Monday August 24th, 2026 — 9am to 12pm CET
In-person: Hannover, Germany — SOUPS 2026
Location: TBD

About

General-purpose AI models enable the generation of non-consensual intimate imagery of adults (AIG-NCII) and child sexual abuse material (AIG-CSAM) with unprecedented ease. Bad actors misuse these capabilities to inflict psychological, financial, and reputational harms on victims/survivors, prompting nations to revise or enact laws prohibiting such misuse. Creating and governing systems resistant to misuse requires solving hard legal, governance, and technical problems. Researchers are tackling these problems from a variety of backgrounds, raising the need of community building to incubate new ideas, help refine existing projects, and build new collaborations that can address the urgent challenges of AI-enabled sexual abuse.

The Community Building for Researchers on Generative AI-Facilitated Image-Based Abuse workshop aims to bring together researchers with backgrounds in usable security, HCI, trust and safety, tech policy, and law that are working on AIG-NCII and AIG-CSAM. The workshop is affiliated to the 22nd Symposium on Usable Privacy and Security (SOUPS 2026) taking place in Hannover, Germany. To participate in the workshop, you will need to submit a research idea that you are working on at the moment or would like to in the future. Authors of successfully peer-reviewed submission will be invited to participate to the workshop and will have the opportunity to present their idea as a lightning talk or a poster. See the Call for ideas below for more information.

Call for ideas

We require a submission of a 1-page PDF containing the abstract of a research idea in one of the following topics:

  • Platform governance and content moderation, and their usability implications for victims/survivors
  • Legal and policy frameworks
  • Technical methods for detection, removal, and prevention
  • Survivor-centered and trauma-informed research methods
  • Psychosocial impacts on victims/survivors
  • Usability of reporting, takedown, and protection tools
  • Ethical and legal barriers to research
  • User perceptions and awareness of AIG-NCII and AIG-CSAM risks
  • Privacy and consent in the design of generative AI systems

We welcome submissions from researchers at all stages, from those with ongoing or published work and practical experience to those entering from adjacent fields. Our goal is to support all researchers seeking to learn about adjacent topics, to find collaborators, or receive feedback from the community.

The workshop will start with a welcome session, followed by a keynote address, we will then transition into lightning presentations. The next portion will incluide small group discussions with a poster session.

Those interested in attending the workshop, but not presenting, are also encouraged to register for the workshop (see below).
