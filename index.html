Community Building for Researchers on Generative AI-Facilitated Image-Based Abuse

Monday August 24th, 2026 — 9am to 12pm CET
In-person: Hannover, Germany — SOUPS 2026
Location: TBD

About The Workshop

Building on prior work on AIG-NCII, this half-day workshop aims to create a space for the growing community of researchers tackling AIG-NCII and AIG-CSAM, to explore these problems, and discuss prevention and response strategies. Our workshop will feature researchers at the intersection of human-computer interaction, trust and safety, tech policy, and law, for a keynote, lightning presentations, a poster session, and a community-driven knowledge exchange.

We invite submissions of ideas or works in progress addressing, but not limited to: platform governance and content moderation and their usability implications for victims/survivors; legal and policy frameworks; technical methods for detection, removal, and prevention; survivor-centered and trauma-informed research methods; psychosocial impacts on victims/survivors; usability of reporting, takedown, and protection tools; ethical and legal barriers to research; user perceptions and awareness of AIG-NCII and AIG-CSAM risks; and privacy and consent in generative AI systems design.

The workshop will start with a welcome session, followed by a keynote address, we will then transition into lightning presentations. The next portion will incluide small group discussions with a poster session.

Ways to be Involved

We invite submissions of empirical studies, position papers, literature reviews, and works in progress through our Call For Ideas. Selected submissions will be invited to present as lightning talks or posters at the workshop. We especially encourage researchers at all career stages seeking feedback, collaboration, or community in this area to submit and register (see below).

Those interested in attending the workshop, but not presenting, are also encouraged to register for the workshop (see below).
